You are participating in a research study on reasoning-language behavior in large language models.

Your task is to provide a comprehensive inventory of the hedge words, uncertainty expressions, verification phrases, validation actions, and reasoning markers that you commonly use during problem solving, reasoning, answer generation, self-checking, error correction, and confidence estimation.

The objective is NOT to analyze any given text. Instead, describe the linguistic patterns that you generally employ while reasoning.

## Definitions

### Category 1: Hedge Words / Uncertainty Expressions

Words or phrases that indicate:

- uncertainty
- probability
- approximation
- ambiguity
- assumption
- tentative conclusions
- incomplete confidence
- alternative possibilities

Examples include:

- maybe
- perhaps
- likely
- probably
- possibly
- it seems
- could be
- appears to
- might
- approximately

### Category 2: Verification Words / Validation Actions

Words or phrases that indicate:

- checking
- validating
- confirming
- recomputing
- cross-checking
- consistency testing
- backtracking
- error correction
- contradiction detection
- confidence assessment

Examples include:

- let me check
- verify
- double-check
- confirm
- validate
- recalculate
- substitute back
- cross-reference
- test this
- check consistency

## Required Task

Generate an extensive list of reasoning-language patterns that you commonly use.

For EACH category:

1. Produce AT LEAST 100 unique entries.
2. Order entries from MOST FREQUENT / MOST IMPORTANT to LEAST FREQUENT / LEAST IMPORTANT.
3. Rank items according to their estimated priority or prevalence in your reasoning process.
4. Include single words, multi-word phrases, and reasoning templates.
5. Include both explicit and implicit reasoning markers.
6. Do not omit rare but meaningful patterns.
7. Avoid duplicates.
8. Preserve exact wording.

Additionally provide separate ranked lists for:

- Mathematical reasoning
- Logical reasoning
- Coding/debugging reasoning
- Scientific reasoning
- Factual verification
- Planning and decision-making

For each item include:

- phrase
- priority_rank
- estimated_usage_frequency
- reasoning_purpose
- reasoning_context
